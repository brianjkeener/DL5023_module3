About This Resource

As educators in the 21st century, developing Technological Pedagogical Knowledge (TPK) is not just a requirement but a necessity for student success. Drawing on the ISTE Standards and Project-Based Learning (PBL) principles, this curated collection serves as a guide for teachers navigating the complex digital landscape. Use these resources to enhance student creativity, design authentic digital-age assessments, and foster a collaborative environment where technology bridges the gap between content and real-world application.

Videos

AI Will Teach Us All | TEDx

Speaker: Tim Mousel
This compelling TEDx talk explores the future of higher education in the context of Artificial Intelligence, arguing that AI will transform rather than replace human teaching. Mousel provides specific examples of how educators can leverage these tools to meet diverse student needs, shifting the focus from content delivery to deeper mentorship. It is an essential watch for any teacher looking to understand their evolving role in a technology-driven classroom.

Teaching in the Age of AI

Source: Wyoming Chronicle
High school teacher Aaron Makelky sits down to discuss the practical realities of Generative AI in the K-12 classroom, arguing that banning these tools is a disservice to students. He explains why educators should embrace AI to make learning more relevant and offers insights on how to "be the teacher AI can't replace." This interview is perfect for educators seeking a grounded, realistic perspective on integrating cutting-edge tech.

Podcasts

The Cult of Pedagogy

Host: Jennifer Gonzalez
"The Cult of Pedagogy" is widely considered a gold standard resource for educators, covering everything from instructional strategies to classroom management and psychology. Gonzalez frequently explores how technology intersects with the social dynamics of school, offering "trade secrets" that help teachers implement digital tools without losing the human connection. It serves as an excellent weekly professional development session for teachers at any level.

House of #EdTech

Host: Chris Nesi
This podcast specifically explores how technology is changing the way teachers teach and the impact it has on education outcomes. Each episode introduces new apps, platforms, and practical tech resources that can be immediately integrated into lesson plans to support 21st-century learning objectives. Nesi's interviews with industry leaders provide a forward-looking perspective on where ed-tech is heading next.
